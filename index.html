Project Rebound

Pitch

In today's workforce, possessing foundational digital skills is crucial. Returning citizens, who are reintegrating into society after incarceration, face significant challenges in finding employment due to the lack of access to technology during their time in prison. Our project focuses on bridging the digital divide and providing returning citizens with the opportunity to acquire essential digital skills. By breaking down barriers, our project aims to empower each one to thrive in the digital age.

Project Background

Problem Statement

Each year, over 600,000 Americans are released from state and federal prisons, but they face higher unemployment rates compared to the national average (ASPE). Digital skills have become increasingly necessary in today's job market, but formerly incarcerated citizens face significant employment challenges due to their limited digital literacy resulting from resource constraints. When formerly incarcerated students begin their college journey, some of them encounter challenges such as include struggling with technical terminologies, staying accountable for their academic responsibilities, comprehending and completing assignments, as well as feeling hesitant to seek assistance. These challenges arise from the need to develop familiarity with technology tasks and improve their basic computer skills. Additionally, some of these students are more vulnerable to online scams and phishing attacks. Moreover, the lack of sufficient support and guidance worsens these difficulties, leading to low retention rates and difficulties in finding employment. These challenges impede their successful integration into society and hinder their ability to overcome obstacles.

Objectives

Our primary goal is to empower returning citizens by providing them with the essential digital skills they need to succeed. To achieve this, our web app focuses on key features, including a language filter for technical terms, a personalized learning path, and topic walkthroughs. The language filter feature enables students with technical terms by providing an alphabetical list and a search function. Visual representations and explanations accompany each term to aid comprehension. Second, the personalized learning path helps students assess their comfort level with technical jargon through an assessment exam. Based on the results, it offers personalized recommendations and courses to support their learning journey. Additionally, the website includes topic walkthroughs, providing step-by-step guidance on specific topics to assist students in their understanding of technology further. These features prioritize improving Project Rebound students' understanding of technology and enhancing their experience in navigating academic requirements.

Future Design Potential

Designers like us should try and reach out to our target users and try to get feedback straight from them. Project Rebound and other projects similar to ours require constant feedback from its users due to the fact that there will always be changes that need to be updated. The design should always be simple and straightforward and combined with feedback, we will be able to minimize any confusion. We also need to be hasty in terms of updating the website as users are using the site around the clock and they may need the site at a moment’s notice. In terms of potential, we could still implement more pages that could incorporate our users' input. For example we can allow users to make their own walkthroughs and have a Project Rebound administrator check whether it’s a good idea to include it or not.

We need to stray away from making design elements confusing for users and making sure that Project Rebound staff as well as the website material is up to date. Because of the fact that we provide help and tutorials to other websites, whenever there is a change on those websites such as a UI change or naming schema change, it needs to be updated on our end as well. It wouldn’t make sense for our tutorials to have outdated information or staff members helping users while they’re not seeing the same things on each end.
